Westwood is dense, but local life is scattered.

Events and resources are spread across flyers, Instagram posts, email lists, group chats, campus channels, calendars, and word of mouth. That makes it too easy for people to miss the very things that could help them feel connected.

The problem

A first-gen student, transfer student, international student, new renter, or young adult in Westwood may be surrounded by people but still have no easy answer to: “What can I go to tonight?”

✓Information is fragmented across too many channels.
✓Many free and low-cost gatherings are hard to discover.
✓Community connection often depends on who already knows who.

The idea

A lightweight public page that turns scattered neighborhood information into one clean guide: a feed, a map, and a simple personal agenda of things happening nearby.

✓See what’s happening tonight.
✓Find events by location, time, and vibe.
✓Save plans and share them with friends.
